CRISPR-Cas9 Gene Editing: Mechanism and Applications

OVERVIEW
CRISPR-Cas9 is a prokaryotic immune defense system repurposed as a precision gene
editing tool. It was first demonstrated as a programmable genome editor in human
cells in 2013 by the Zhang lab at the Broad Institute and simultaneously by the
Doudna-Charpentier group. The system enables targeted double-strand breaks (DSBs)
at specific genomic loci with single-nucleotide precision.

CORE COMPONENTS
The Cas9 protein is an RNA-guided endonuclease derived from Streptococcus pyogenes
(SpCas9). It has two nuclease domains: HNH, which cleaves the strand complementary
to the guide RNA, and RuvC, which cleaves the non-complementary strand. Both
domains must be active to produce a blunt-ended double-strand break.

The guide RNA (gRNA) is a synthetic fusion of two naturally occurring RNA molecules:
the CRISPR RNA (crRNA) and the trans-activating crRNA (tracrRNA). The spacer region
of the crRNA (20 nucleotides) is designed to match the target DNA sequence. The
tracrRNA scaffold anchors the complex to Cas9.

The protospacer adjacent motif (PAM) is a short DNA sequence (5'-NGG-3' for SpCas9)
immediately downstream of the target site on the non-template strand. PAM recognition
is required for Cas9 binding and cleavage. Sequences lacking a PAM cannot be cleaved
by SpCas9 regardless of gRNA complementarity.

MECHANISM OF ACTION
1. The gRNA is loaded into Cas9, forming a ribonucleoprotein (RNP) complex.
2. The RNP complex scans double-stranded DNA for PAM sequences.
3. On PAM detection, Cas9 locally unwinds the DNA and checks for complementarity
   between the gRNA spacer and the protospacer.
4. If complementarity is sufficient (seed region: bases 1-12 proximal to PAM are
   most critical), Cas9 undergoes conformational activation.
5. HNH cleaves the complementary strand; RuvC cleaves the non-complementary strand,
   producing a blunt DSB 3 base pairs upstream of the PAM.

DNA REPAIR PATHWAYS AFTER CLEAVAGE
Non-Homologous End Joining (NHEJ): The dominant repair pathway in most cell types.
NHEJ is error-prone and frequently introduces insertions or deletions (indels) at
the cut site. Indels in a coding exon typically cause frameshift mutations leading
to premature stop codons and loss-of-function alleles. NHEJ efficiency is high
(often >70% editing frequency in cell culture).

Homology-Directed Repair (HDR): An alternative pathway that uses a provided DNA
template to introduce precise edits. HDR requires a homology donor (single-stranded
oligonucleotide or plasmid) and is most efficient in S/G2 phase of the cell cycle.
HDR efficiency is substantially lower than NHEJ in most contexts (typically 1-10%
without cell cycle synchronization).

BASE EDITING AND PRIME EDITING
Base editors are Cas9 variants fused to deaminase enzymes. Cytosine base editors
(CBEs) convert C•G to T•A; adenine base editors (ABEs) convert A•T to G•C. Base
editors do not create DSBs and have a narrower editing window (approximately 4-8
nucleotides within the protospacer).

Prime editing uses a nickase Cas9 (nCas9, with RuvC domain inactivated) fused to
a reverse transcriptase. The prime editing guide RNA (pegRNA) contains both the
spacer sequence and an RT template encoding the desired edit. Prime editing can
install all 12 types of point mutations, small insertions, and small deletions
without DSBs or donor templates.

OFF-TARGET EFFECTS
Cas9 can bind and cleave sequences with up to 5 mismatches to the gRNA, particularly
if mismatches are in the PAM-distal region (bases 13-20 of the spacer). Off-target
cleavage at genomic sites bearing partial complementarity is a key safety concern
for clinical applications. High-fidelity SpCas9 variants (eSpCas9, SpCas9-HF1,
HypaCas9) reduce off-target activity by weakening non-specific DNA contacts while
preserving on-target efficiency.

DELIVERY METHODS
Plasmid DNA transfection: High off-target risk due to prolonged Cas9 expression.
Viral vectors (AAV, lentivirus): High efficiency in vivo; AAV has a 4.7 kb cargo
limit that constrains SpCas9 (4.2 kb coding sequence) delivery with gRNA.
Ribonucleoprotein (RNP) electroporation: Preferred for ex vivo editing; transient
Cas9 exposure reduces off-target events; RNP degrades within 24-48 hours.
Lipid nanoparticles (LNPs): Emerging preferred route for in vivo delivery of mRNA-
encoded Cas9; used in clinical trials for transthyretin amyloidosis (NTLA-2001).

CLINICAL APPLICATIONS
Sickle cell disease and beta-thalassemia: BCL11A enhancer disruption reactivates
fetal hemoglobin (HbF) expression. Casgevy (exagamglogene autotemcel), approved
by the FDA in December 2023, is the first CRISPR therapy approved for human use.
It uses ex vivo editing of patient hematopoietic stem cells.

Transthyretin amyloidosis: NTLA-2001 targets the TTR gene in liver hepatocytes
using LNP-delivered Cas9 mRNA. Phase 1 data showed >90% reduction in serum
transthyretin levels after a single dose.

KEY CONSTRAINTS
- SpCas9 PAM requirement (NGG) limits targetable sites to roughly 1 per 8 bp in
  the human genome on average.
- The 20-nt gRNA spacer must avoid self-complementarity to prevent hairpin formation.
- GC content of the spacer between 40-70% is associated with higher editing efficiency.
- Chromatin accessibility at the target locus significantly affects editing efficiency;
  heterochromatic regions are less efficiently edited.
